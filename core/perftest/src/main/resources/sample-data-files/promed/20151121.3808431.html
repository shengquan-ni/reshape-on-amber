The Caldas Territorial Health Office reports that there are now 12 cases of Zika virus [infection] in the department. The Municipality with the most cases is Marmato.

To date 8 cases have been recorded in Marmato, 2 in La Dorada, 1 in Chinchina, and 1 in Riosucio.

Zika virus [infection] is [transmitted] by mosquito bite, and is caused by a virus classified as an arbovirus in the flavivirus genus, in which mosquitoes and tick [vectors] are a part [or transmission].

According the Caldas Territorial Health Office, Zika [virus infections] present with severe joint pains, high fever and conjunctivitis.

[The reported severe joint pains make the infection sound more like chikungunya virus, which is also circulating in many parts of Colombia. No mention is made of whether these cases are laboratory confirmed or were diagnosed clinically. Laboratory confirmation is essential, because the symptoms of Zika, chikungunya, and dengue viruses are similar enough that diagnosis on clinical grounds is difficult, and these viruses are all co-circulating in the several Colombian departments.

Maps of Colombia can be accessed at http://www.lib.utexas.edu/maps/americas/colombia_pol_2001.jpg and http://healthmap.org/promed/p/16. - Mod.TY]
