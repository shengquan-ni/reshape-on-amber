Cases in various countries:
Brazil microcephaly
- National. 8 Dec 2015. (susp.) as of 5 Dec 2015, the Ministry of Health announced 1761 cases of microcephaly in 422 municipalities in 13 states and the Federal District.

The balance shows 19 babies with microcephaly died in 8 states: Pernambuco 804 cases, Paraiba 316 cases (with 1 death), Bahia 180 cases (with 2 deaths), Rio Grande do Norte 106 cases (with 7 deaths), Sergipe 96 cases (with 4 deaths), Alagoas 81 cases, Ceara 40 cases (with 1 death); Maranhao 37 cases (with 1 death), Piaui 36 cases (with 1 death), Tocantins 29 cases, Rio de Janeiro 23 cases (with 2 deaths), Mato Grosso do Sul 9 cases, Goias 3 cases, Distrito Federal 1 case.
http://noticias.uol.com.br/saude/ultimas-noticias/redacao/2015/12/08/casos-de-microcefalia-chegam-a-1761-e-atingem-422-municipios-diz-governo.htm [in Portuguese]

[An earlier unofficial report indicated that there are cases in 18 states. The official Ministry of Health report above indicates that there are cases in 13 states plus the Federal District.

Maps of Brazil can be accessed at http://s13.postimg.org/jumnalk87/map_of_Brazil.gif and http://healthmap.org/promed/p/6. - Mod.TY]

- New protocol for microcephaly related to Zika virus infection. 8 Dec 2015. The Secretariat for Health Surveillance of the Ministry of Health issued the following protocol [to document cases of microcephaly possibly related to Zika virus infection]:
1. Pregnant woman with possible Zika virus infection during gestation.
2. Fetus with changes in head circumference possibly related to the infection.
3. Spontaneous abortion due to possible association with Zika virus infection during gestation.
4. Neonatal death due to possible association with Zika virus infection during gestation.
5. Recently born alive with microcephaly possibly associated with Zika virus infection during gestation.

The full protocol is available at
http://portalsaude.saude.gov.br/images/pdf/2015/dezembro/08/microcefalia-protocolo-de-vigilancia-e-resposta-v1.pdf [in Portuguese]

- Sao Paulo state. 9 Dec 2015. Although the state does not require notification of microcephaly cases, 5 municipalities have reported 18 suspected cases: Campinas 10 cases, Sao Paulo 4 cases, Santo Andre 2 cases, Guarulhos 1 case, Olimpia 1 case. There is no confirmation of Zika virus infection, but samples have been sent to the Instituto Adolfo Lutz, the state reference laboratory for testing.
http://noticias.uol.com.br/saude/ultimas-noticias/estado/2015/12/09/estado-de-sp-nao-notifica-suspeitas-de-microcefalia.htm [in Portuguese]

[Another 9 Dec 2015 report quotes the Sao Paulo state Secretary of Health as stating that there are 54 cases of microcephaly in the state, a number that is within the mean annual number of cases within the past 5 years. None of the current cases of microcephaly are confirmed as associated with Zika virus infections (http://g1.globo.com/sao-paulo/noticia/2015/12/secretario-diz-que-casos-em-sp-de-microcefalia-estao-dentro-da-media.html in Portuguese). Mod. TY]

Colombia infections (Caldas department). 6 Dec 2015. (registered) 13 cases. Municipalities affected: Marmato 8 cases, La Dorada 2 cases, Chinchina 1 case, Viterbo 1 case, Riosucio 1 case.
http://caracol.com.co/emisora/2015/12/06/manizales/1449412094_256954.html [in Spanish]

[There is just one additional case of Zika virus infection reported over the past 3 weeks, so it does not appear that the infection is spreading very rapidly here.

Maps of Colombia can be accessed at http://www.lib.utexas.edu/maps/americas/colombia_pol_2001.jpg and http://healthmap.org/promed/p/11957. - Mod.TY]

Panama infections (Ustupu island, Alligandi district, Guna Yala province [formerly San Blas]) WHO. 5 Dec 2015. (susp.) 68 patients with fever and rash; blood samples from 43 (of whom 30 symptomatic), laboratory negative for dengue and chikungunya virus; 3 samples positive for Zika virus by RT-PCR.
http://www.who.int/csr/don/05-december-2015-zika-panama/en/

[This report provides additional information about suspected case numbers not contained in the previous update.

Maps of Panama can be accessed at http://pedasitours.com/blueberry/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/political_map.gif and http://healthmap.org/promed/p/49258; a map showing the location of Ustupu Island can be seen at http://mapcarta.com/19746206. - Mod.TY]

El Salvador infections. 9 Dec 2015. (susp.) 31 Oct-6 Nov 2015, 1184 cases according to the Salvador Institute for Social Security; (susp.) 29 Nov-5 Dec 2015, 738 cases attended but 864 cases total and among them 5 pregnant women, according to the Ministry of Health in the latest epidemiological bulletin. The ministry has sent samples to the USA CDC for confirmation.
http://www.elsalvador.com/articulo/nacional/triplican-casos-zika-95606 [in Spanish]

[It is a bit difficult to reconcile the difference in case counts, but it may be due to different reporting dates, with a delay in reporting from the Ministry.

Maps of El Salvador can be accessed at http://www.lib.utexas.edu/maps/americas/elsalvador.jpg and http://healthmap.org/promed/p/21. - Mod.TY]
