Cases in various countries:
Mexico and Central America
---
Mexico (national). 22 Dec 2015. (conf.) as of week 48 ending 5 Dec 2015, 2 cases locally acquired.
https://www.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/noticia/1181121.chikungunya-zika-ebola-y-dengue-marcaron-2015.html [in Spanish]

[A HealthMap/ProMED-mail map of Mexico can be accessed at http://healthmap.org/promed/p/14. - Mod.TY]

Panama. 22 Dec 2015. (susp.) 91 cases, (conf.) 4 cases locally acquired; Localities affected: Ustupu and Ogobsugun islands, Guna Yala province, where dengue virus is co-circulating.
http://www.who.int/csr/don/22-december-2015-zika-panama/en/

[A 29 Dec 2015 report indicates that the confirmed case number is now up to 6 and a health emergency has been declared for the area (http://www.telemetro.com/nacionales/registran-Guna-Yala-declarado-epdimeiologica_0_875612998.html [in Spanish]).

Maps of Panama can be accessed at http://pedasitours.com/blueberry/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/political_map.gif and http://healthmap.org/promed/p/18. - Mod.TY]

Caribbean
---
Puerto Rico. 18 Dec 2015. (reported) 1 locally acquired case.
http://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/notices/watch/zika-virus-puerto-rico

[A map showing the location of Puerto Rico in the Caribbean can be accessed at http://www.worldatlas.com/webimage/countrys/namerica/caribb/pr.htm. - Mod.TY]

Martinique. 25 Dec 2015. (reported) locally acquired but case numbers not given.
http://stluciatimes.com/2015/12/25/martinique-reports-local-zika-virus-transmission

[A 19 Dec 2015 report indicated that there is one case (http://www.notretemps.com/sante/cas-confirmane-et-en,i101590es-de-zika-en-guy [in French]).

Maps showing the location of Martinique in the Caribbean can be accessed at http://www.worldatlas.com/webimage/countrys/namerica/caribb/martnque.htm and http://healthmap.org/promed/p/43638. - Mod.TY]

Germany ex Haiti. 29 Dec 2015. A patient just returned from Haiti, where she became ill on 16 Dec 2015 with fever and joint pain. The patient was working in Port-au-Prince for the last 7 months. The infection was serologically confirmed by the Tropical Institute (Bernhard-Nocht-Institut) in Hamburg.

[ProMED thanks Dr. Nels Schuebel <n.schuebel@gmx.de> of Klinikum Osnabruck, Germany for submitting this report.

Maps of Haiti can be accessed at http://www.worldatlas.com/webimage/countrys/namerica/caribb/ht.htm and http://www.corpra.org/images/haiti-map.gif. - Mod.TY]

South America
---
Brazil:
- Microcephaly. 29 Dec 2015. (susp.) as of 26 Dec 2015, 2972 cases in 656 municipalities in 19 states and the Federal District; Deaths (susp.) 40; States with most cases: Alagoas 129 cases, Bahia 271 cases, Ceara 134 cases, Pariba 476 cases, Pernambuco 1153 cases, Rio Grande do Norte 154 cases, Sergipe 146 cases, Maranhao 94 cases, Piaui 51 cases.
http://portalsaude.saude.gov.br/index.php/cidadao/principal/agencia-saude/21459-saude-divulga-dados-atualizados-de-microcefalia [in Portuguese]

[A 19 Dec 2015 report indicates that annual case numbers of microcephaly 2010-2014, previous to arrival of Zika virus, was 139-167 cases (http://portalsaude.saude.gov.br/index.php/o-ministerio/principal/secretarias/svs/noticias-svs/21367-saude-alerta-viajantes-para-eliminacao-dos-criadouros-do-aedes-aegypi [in Portuguese]).

A 24 Dec 2015 report indicated that 6 states have declared a health state of emergency due to a high number of microcephaly cases (http://newsworldindia.in/world/brazil-declares-emergency-after-over-2400-babies-are-born-with-brain-disorder/164280/).

Maps of Brazil can be accessed at http://s13.postimg.org/jumnalk87/map_of_Brazil.gif and http://healthmap.org/promed/p/6. - Mod.TY]

- Ribeirao Preto, Sao Paulo State. 24 Dec 2015. (susp.) 8 cases, (conf.) 2 cases that occurred in May and June 2015, but recently confirmed.
http://g1.globo.com/sp/ribeirao-preto-franca/noticia/2015/12/ribeirao-preto-confirma-dois-casos-de-zika-virus-diz-prefeitura.html [in Portuguese]

Colombia (national). 26 Dec 2015. (susp.) more than 5700 cases, (conf.) 1000 cases; Region most affected: Atlantic [Caribbean].
http://www.minuto30.com/ministerio-de-salud-reporto-mas-de-6-700-casos-sospechosos-del-virus-del-zika/420343/ [in Spanish]

[Maps of Colombia can be accessed at http://www.lib.utexas.edu/maps/americas/colombia_pol_2001.jpg and http://healthmap.org/promed/p/16. - Mod.TY]
